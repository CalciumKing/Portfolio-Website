Landen Ingerslev

(Placeholder Image)

My name is Landen Ingerslev, a student at Old Colony Regional Vocational Technical High School, studying computer science. With knowledge of over 10 different programming languages, my passion is learning new aspects of computer science to further expand skill sets. Challenges represent opportunities for growth, and tackling them with maximum effort and hard work is essential, especially in areas related to computer science.

A strong foundation in general computer science skills has been established, with advanced expertise in Java. This knowledge allows for the translation of large scripts into other languages. I often spend time converting Java programs into Python to deepen my understanding of both. Currently, there is a focus on teaching myself cybersecurity skills in Java and writing scripts with new ideas on the subject.

My future plans include entering the cybersecurity field. Alongside this interest, skills in both programming and web design have been developed. Furthermore, my college plans involve studying computer science, machine learning, and cybersecurity and I hope to pursue a career in computer science.

Interests extend beyond the field of computer science. Visits to the local gym and skateboarding and biking around the neighborhood are daily activities. In winter, snowboarding occurs monthly. An interest in linguistics and a basic understanding of several complex languages are some other of my personal hobbies. Traveling has also been a passion, with experiences in various countries around the world and multiple states across the U.S.

Putting in maximum effort is of utmost importance, ensuring that every endeavor is approached with dedication. Regardless of interest in a task, there’s always a commitment to completing it efficiently and thoroughly. If something is done incorrectly or can be improved, I will retry until it not only meets, but exceeds expectations.

Four years at Old Colony have strengthened both computer science and academic skills. I am excited to apply these skills to further education and eventually obtain a career within the computer science field.
